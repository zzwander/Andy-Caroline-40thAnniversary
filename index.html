Our Story

Andy and Caroline met for the first time at Campbell Hall at the start of their UCL university life in early October 1978. Andy, a rugby-playing West Country boy from Almondsbury via Rudgeway; Caroline, from Burnley of a very diverse background - Austrian mum and Jamaican dad who met after the war and for some strange reason settled in Burnley. Despite different backgrounds, they both had loving parents with strong values and shared the pain of growing up in the 70s; the 3 day week, inflation of 25%, job losses and crappy governments (just like now).

They were clearly destined to meet – shared Northern roots (Andy’s mum), he used to follow Burnley FC as a boy and so did she; she shared room 256 and he shared room 356 (so she could hear his noise); she studied German and he studied Geography (the two “Gs”). They were good friends for the year sharing a love of Camel cigarettes and all-night course work sessions and after one momentous evening, finally became more than friends and got engaged at the White Hart pub in Littleton Upon Severn 2 weeks later to the utter shock and disbelief of everybody. They said it would never last...

They learned about each other living together in crappy London flats and even spent a year apart during Caroline’s year in Germany. They kept close through reverse charge calls booked at the German Post Office, lots of letters and the occasional trip to Landshut or back home. They eventually got married in April 1983, shortly after Caroline graduated in 1982 and started their careers as trainee-chartered accountants (lasted a year!)

Their wedding day was the only sunny day in April and the bride’s hair was ginger (hairdresser catastrophe the week before) and groom was hungover from his stag night with his uncles the night before but it was such a special day even when the vicar congratulated Caroline on her marriage to David.

Andy and Caroline Contra Mundum (def. against the world; in defiance of all general opinion)

They adopted this motto after Andy read Brideshead Revisited. It has served them well – a wonderful 40 years with the most important highlights being Charlotte and Henry born in 1988 and 1993. They moved around - from North London to Kent and then Hertfordshire and now Switzerland and after long careers in banking (Andy) and international HR (Caroline) are now happily retired having learned during the pandemic that they still love, like and laugh with each other.
